The Shepherd King

What Are You Waiting For?

This is an outline for a long term project I want to make for fun. It would be a historical fiction of a Biblical narrative of David. Long story short- he had a crazy life which I thought would make for a beautiful narrative. Courage, dedication, friendship, loss, responsibility, failure, repentance, betrayal, and reconciliation. It's a touching story arch that I thought could be a wholistic narrative- and a good game. The game would be a 2D rpg with a focus on story telling.

Design

The following is an outline of the gerneral mechanics and design of the game. Questions like game design and character development are addressed here. Pacing for the story and character scaling are also discussed.

Intro Funnel

Beginning area that is small, confined, and teaches you the base mechanics.

Funnel down into a very narrow area with little choices.

Expand drastically with a reveal that feels rewarding.

Pacing

Enemy leveling.

Character leveling.

Story beats that feel very well paced- not drawn out.

Character Development

Every subplot brings something of use.

Making sure every main character gets a strong story.

Main Character Scaling

Types of weapons change as you play, up until second chapter.

Types of attacks based on how much you use the weapon OR based on player choice- what they want to upgrade.

Character skill tree.

Secret weapons revolving around Biblical character.

Character skill tree

Courage: Courage increases the chances of hitting the enemy twice or thrice depending on how high the courage stat is buffed.

Strength: Makes the damage input increase as you increase it.

Defense: Increases the defense.

Health: Increases the health of the character as buffed.

Charm: Makes the harp or similar weapons more powerful/effective. Makes shop prices decrease, or makes the give discounts.

Speed: Makes the main character attack first. If speed doubles or triples the enemy, multiple turns can be taken before the enemy.

Weapon Classes

Staff: An attack that hits harder than the sling but can only accesses enemies in the front. A weaker sword.

Sling: A long ranged attack that is a weaker form of the bow.

Sword: A heavy hitting attack that only accesses enemies in the front.

Bow: Long range attack that hits characters inaccessible in the back.

Harp: Puts enemies to sleep / lowers defense.

Intro

Chapter 1: A Shepherd Boy

[The game opens with a cinematic sequence where Samuel, an aged prophet, narrates the story of David, setting the stage for the player.]

In the vast expanse of eternity, from the dawning of creation to the very core of existence, the tapestry of human history unfolds in a symphony of triumph and tragedy. Across the annals of time, from the humble beginnings of mankind in the garden, to this very day, one truth remains constant: the fragility of the human spirit, the inevitability of our fallibility.

Throughout all of time, from the dawn of creation to the present moment, humanity has grappled with the dual nature of our existence. We are beings of light and darkness, capable of both boundless compassion and unfathomable cruelty. In the grand epic of life, we dance upon the stage of destiny, each of us playing our part in the unfolding story that is our life.

Yet, in our moments of weakness and folly, in the depths of despair and doubt, there shines a beacon of hope—a divine thread woven into the fabric of our being. For even amidst the chaos and confusion of mortal existence, there exists a plan— a grand design.

In the end, it is not the greatness of our deeds or the magnitude of our failures that define us but rather the courage to rise above our limitations, to embrace our destiny with open arms, and to walk boldly into the unknown, guided by faith and fueled by hope. For in the end, it is not the journey itself that matters but rather the lessons we learn along the way, the bonds we forge, and the legacy we leave behind.


Act 1

[As Samuel's voice fades, the scene transitions seamlessly into gameplay, with the player taking control of David amidst the lush landscapes of ancient Israel. The camera zooms in on David as he confronts a fierce lion in the wilderness.]

[The player engages in turn-based combat, strategically selecting David's actions and abilities to outmaneuver the lion. Each move requires careful consideration, as David's health and stamina are finite resources. The battle system is designed to be intuitive yet challenging, rewarding players for tactical thinking and mastery of their character's skills.]

[After a thrilling battle, David emerges victorious, showcasing his bravery and prowess in combat with a sling. The lion's defeated roar echoes across the landscape as David catches his breath, the adrenaline still coursing through his veins.]

[As David explores the surrounding area, the player discovers hidden treasures, encounters other wildlife, and interacts with NPCs who offer quests and challenges. The environment is teeming with life, from lush forests to babbling brooks, inviting players to immerse themselves in the richly detailed world.]

[Suddenly, one of David's brothers rushes up to him, breathless and urgent. David returns home to find Samuel waiting for him, a solemn expression on his face. With a sense of reverence, Samuel anoints David with a horn of oil, proclaiming him as the chosen one, destined to be the future king of Israel. The moment is both awe-inspiring and surreal, as if time itself has paused to mark this significant event.]

[Despite the weight of his destiny, David continues to live his normal life, engaging in quests and sidequests that range from mundane tasks to epic adventures. The player can explore the bustling marketplace, barter with merchants for rare items, and participate in friendly competitions with fellow villagers.]

[As time passes in the game, the player experiences pivotal moments in David's life, such as conversations with his father Jesse and his brothers. These interactions provide insight into David's relationships and the dynamics within his family, adding depth to the narrative.]

[When night falls, the player can choose to have David retire to his humble abode, signaling the end of the day and allowing the plot to progress to the next chapter. With its blend of immersive gameplay, strategic combat, and rich storytelling, the first chapter of the game sets the stage for an epic adventure that will captivate players for hours on end.]
